Hello, I'm Charlie.

I graduated from Washington University in St. Louis with a Master's degree in Computer Science. My primary interests focus on large-scale machine learning systems, with a particular emphasis on distributed large model inference, heterogeneous computing, and system performance optimization.

I have a long-standing interest in system efficiency issues within real-world deployment environments, especially the complex interactions between computation, memory, and communication, and how these factors collectively impact throughput, scalability, and resource utilization. Centered around these issues, I typically combine performance profiling, experimental analysis, and system mechanism design to translate complex system challenges into measurable, verifiable, and optimizable problems.

More broadly, I maintain a continuous interest in problems at the intersection of machine learning systems, high-performance computing, and real-world infrastructure. I also hope that the systems I participate in building are not only meaningful in research but also operate stably and efficiently in practical environments.
